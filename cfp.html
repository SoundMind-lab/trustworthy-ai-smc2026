Call for Papers

Artificial intelligence is increasingly deployed in safety-critical environments such as healthcare monitoring, disaster response, and information authenticity verification. While modern machine learning models achieve high predictive performance, significant challenges remain in ensuring robustness, reliability, interpretability, and human trust in real-world deployments.

This workshop aims to bring together researchers and practitioners working on trustworthy AI systems for safety-critical perception and decision-making. The workshop will encourage interdisciplinary discussion across AI, cyber-physical systems, human-AI interaction, and real-world AI deployment.

Topics of Interest

Topics include, but are not limited to:

  • Uncertainty estimation and confidence calibration in machine learning
  • Explainable AI for safety-critical decision making
  • Human-AI trust and interaction
  • Robust perception under noisy or incomplete data
  • Failure detection and anomaly awareness
  • AI for medical and physiological monitoring
  • Disaster detection and damage assessment
  • Deepfake and media authenticity detection
  • Human-in-the-loop learning and decision support
  • Deployment challenges for real-world AI systems
Publication: Accepted papers will be included in the IEEE SMC conference proceedings and published in IEEE Xplore.
